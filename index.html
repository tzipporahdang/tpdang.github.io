About

Hello! I am Tzipporah (pronounced: “Zipora”), a PhD candidate at the University of Delaware in the Impression Formation Social Neuroscience Lab.

I am a quantitative researcher with experience leading research in impression formation, social cognition, and behavior. My goal is to leverage my skills in research methodology and data interpretation in applied settings.

I currently study impression formation and person perception, focusing on the influence of background knowledge and perceptual cues on judgments. I have expertise in managing the end-to-end details of entire research programs including study design (semi-formal interviews, observation, surveys, A/B testing), participant recruitment, data collection, data cleaning and analysis, and disseminating results.

I enjoy olive-free foods, the TV shows Bob's Burgers and We Bare Bears, and novice gardening. I have two very good pups, Monty and Hunter.
